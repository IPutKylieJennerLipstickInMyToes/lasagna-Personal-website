Conservation of Endangered Species

About Endangered Species

Endangered species are those at risk of extinction due to habitat loss, poaching, climate change, and other human activities. Protecting these species is vital for maintaining biodiversity and ecological balance.

Examples of Endangered Species

  • Amur Leopard: Fewer than 100 left in the wild.
  • Sumatran Orangutan: Critically endangered due to habitat loss.
  • Vaquita: A small porpoise with fewer than 30 individuals remaining.
  • Snow Leopard: Facing threats from poaching and habitat degradation.

How You Can Help

There are many ways to contribute to conservation efforts:

  • Support wildlife conservation organizations.
  • Educate others about the importance of biodiversity.
  • Participate in local conservation programs.
  • Reduce your ecological footprint.
